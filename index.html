Why DNS Matters

DNS is a foundational service of the modern internet. It translates human-friendly domain names into machine-readable IP addresses so users can reach websites and online services without memorizing long strings of numbers.

Although DNS is often called the “phonebook of the internet,” it also supports performance, scalability, redundancy, security, and privacy across the global internet.

Without DNS, users would need to remember numerical IP addresses to access websites, making the internet far less accessible.

Abstract

DNS, or the Domain Name System, is a foundational internet technology that allows domain names to be translated into IP addresses. This website introduces the role and purpose of DNS, explains its historical development, shows how DNS resolution works, and highlights modern concerns around security, privacy, and resilience.
